## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 Vision

To become the single platform where a job seeker moves from "I don't know how to present myself" to "I am ready to be hired" — combining resume creation, AI-driven optimization, cover letter generation, interview practice, and application tracking into one connected, intelligent workflow instead of five disconnected tools.

### 1.2 Mission

CareerForge's mission is to remove the guesswork, anxiety, and inefficiency from the modern job search by giving every candidate — regardless of background, university, or access to career coaching — the same caliber of resume optimization, ATS insight, and interview preparation that was historically reserved for candidates with paid coaches or insider networks.

### 1.3 Product Overview

CareerForge is a SaaS web application that allows a registered user to:

- Build ATS-friendly resumes using guided templates and a live-editing builder.
- Run an AI-powered ATS Scanner that scores a resume against a specific job description and highlights missing keywords, formatting risks, and structural weaknesses.
- Generate tailored, AI-written cover letters mapped to a specific job description and company.
- Practice AI-driven mock interviews (HR, technical, behavioral, resume-based, and JD-based) with per-answer scoring and improvement suggestions.
- Track every job application through a Kanban-style Job Tracker from "Wishlist" through "Offer" or "Rejected."
- View a unified Dashboard summarizing resume health, application pipeline, and interview readiness.
- Operate on a freemium subscription model with an AI-credit system that gates usage of compute-intensive AI features.

An Admin Dashboard supports internal operations: user management, subscription oversight, AI usage monitoring, analytics, and content moderation of user feedback.

### 1.4 Target Audience

| Segment | Description |
|---|---|
| University students | Preparing for internships/campus placements, first-time resume builders |
| Freshers / recent graduates | 0–2 years experience, applying broadly, need ATS guidance |
| Experienced professionals | 2+ years experience, targeting career transitions or promotions, need tailored resumes per role |
| Career switchers | Professionals moving between industries or functions who need repositioning help |
| (Future) Recruiters / Talent teams | Reviewing candidate resumes and interview readiness scores (Phase 3+) |

### 1.5 Business Value

- **Recurring revenue** via tiered subscriptions (Free / Premium) and an AI-credit consumption model.
- **High engagement loop**: resume creation → ATS scan → cover letter → interview practice → job tracking creates multiple weekly touchpoints, improving retention over single-use resume tools.
- **Defensible data moat**: aggregated, anonymized data on which resume patterns and interview answers correlate with successful outcomes can improve AI models over time and inform premium insights products.
- **Low marginal cost, high perceived value**: AI-generated content (resumes, cover letters, interview feedback) scales without linear increases in headcount.
- **Expansion surface**: a natural upsell path exists into recruiter-facing tools, enterprise/university licensing, and career-coaching marketplaces (see Section 18, Future Scope).

### 1.6 Competitive Landscape (Context)

The market includes point solutions that each solve one slice of the job-search problem: standalone resume builders focused primarily on templates and design; standalone ATS-checker tools that provide a score but little actionable guidance; standalone AI interview practice apps with no connection to a candidate's actual resume or target role; and manual spreadsheet-based job trackers. CareerForge's differentiation is not any single feature in isolation, but the connected pipeline: the job description a user pastes into the ATS Scanner is the same job description that powers the Cover Letter Generator and the AI Interview module, and the resume a user builds is the same resume that gets scored, tailored, and linked to a Job Tracker entry. This shared-context architecture is a deliberate product decision that should inform engineering's data model (Section 14) — job descriptions, resumes, and cover letters are first-class, cross-referenced entities, not siloed per-feature blobs.

### 1.7 Problems Being Solved

1. **ATS rejection blindness** — Most candidates have no visibility into why their resume is filtered out by Applicant Tracking Systems before a human ever sees it.
2. **Generic, un-tailored applications** — Candidates reuse one resume/cover letter for every job, reducing match quality and response rates.
3. **Interview anxiety from lack of practice** — Realistic, low-stakes interview practice with actionable feedback is expensive or unavailable to most candidates.
4. **Fragmented job search tooling** — Resume builders, cover letter tools, interview prep apps, and spreadsheets for tracking applications are typically separate products with no shared context.
5. **Inconsistent resume quality** — Many candidates, especially students and freshers, do not know standard resume conventions (structure, action verbs, quantification of impact).
6. **Lack of feedback loops** — Traditional resume/interview prep offers no measurable, iterative scoring system to show a candidate they are improving.

## 2. Product Goals

### 2.1 Primary Goals

1. Enable a first-time user to create a complete, ATS-friendly resume in under 15 minutes.
2. Provide an ATS compatibility score with actionable, specific feedback within 30 seconds of scan submission.
3. Generate a tailored cover letter from a job description in under 20 seconds of AI processing time.
4. Deliver a realistic AI mock interview experience with per-answer feedback comparable in usefulness to a human career coach's first-pass review.
5. Give users a single dashboard view of their entire job search health (resume quality, applications, interview readiness) at a glance.
6. Convert a meaningful percentage of free users into paying Premium subscribers through clearly demonstrated value (not dark patterns).

### 2.2 Secondary Goals

1. Reduce resume creation time for returning users (editing an existing resume) to under 5 minutes.
2. Support multi-resume management so users can maintain role-specific resume variants.
3. Build a feedback and analytics loop that allows the product team to identify which AI suggestions correlate with better user outcomes.
4. Establish an admin toolset that lets a small operations team manage a user base of 100,000+ without manual database intervention.
5. Ensure the platform is accessible (WCAG 2.1 AA) so it is usable by candidates with disabilities, including screen-reader users relying on the resume builder.

### 2.3 Success Metrics

| Metric | Definition | Target (12 months post-launch) |
|---|---|---|
| Activation Rate | % of signups who complete a resume within 7 days | ≥ 60% |
| ATS Scan Adoption | % of resume owners who run at least 1 ATS scan | ≥ 70% |
| Interview Practice Adoption | % of active users who complete ≥ 1 mock interview | ≥ 40% |
| Free-to-Paid Conversion | % of free users upgrading to Premium within 30 days | ≥ 5% |
| Weekly Active Users (WAU) / Monthly Active Users (MAU) | Stickiness ratio | ≥ 25% |
| Resume Export Success Rate | % of PDF exports completed without error | ≥ 99.5% |
| AI Response Latency (P95) | Time from AI request to first token/response | ≤ 8 seconds |
| Churn Rate (Premium) | Monthly subscription cancellation rate | ≤ 5% |
| NPS | Net Promoter Score from in-app survey | ≥ 40 |
| Support Ticket Rate | Tickets per 1,000 MAU | ≤ 15 |

### 2.4 KPIs (Business-Level)

- Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) growth rate.
- Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) vs. Lifetime Value (LTV) ratio (target ≥ 3:1).
- Average AI credits consumed per active user per month (cost-control indicator).
- Application-to-Interview conversion rate self-reported by users in the Job Tracker (proxy for product effectiveness).
- Time-to-first-value (signup → first completed resume).

## 3. User Personas

### 3.1 Persona: The Student ("Ananya, 20, Junior in Computer Science")

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goals** | Land a summer internship; build first-ever resume; understand what recruiters expect; practice for campus placement interviews. |
| **Pain Points** | No prior work experience to list; unsure how to phrase academic projects as accomplishments; intimidated by interview format; limited access to career counseling. |
| **Usage Pattern** | Bursty usage around campus placement season; heavy use of templates and AI suggestions; frequent resume edits; low willingness to pay (price-sensitive, prefers Free plan). |
| **Technical Skills** | Comfortable with web apps and mobile; not familiar with resume/ATS conventions. |

### 3.2 Persona: The Fresher ("Rohan, 23, Recent Graduate, 0–1 Years Experience")

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goals** | Convert internship/academic experience into a professional resume; apply to many jobs quickly; understand why applications aren't getting responses. |
| **Pain Points** | Generic resume gets filtered by ATS; doesn't know how to tailor a resume per job; anxious in first "real" interviews; needs help articulating impact without much experience. |
| **Usage Pattern** | High-frequency user during active job search (daily); heavy Job Tracker usage; runs ATS scans against many job postings; likely to convert to Premium during active search. |
| **Technical Skills** | Digitally native; moderate familiarity with productivity tools; not expert in resume writing. |

### 3.3 Persona: The Experienced Professional ("Priya, 31, Product Manager, 7 Years Experience")

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goals** | Reposition resume for a career pivot or promotion; maintain multiple tailored resume versions per target role; polish executive-level cover letters; practice senior-level behavioral interviews. |
| **Pain Points** | Limited time to manually tailor resumes for each application; needs to quantify complex achievements concisely; wants higher-quality, nuanced AI output than generic tools provide. |
| **Usage Pattern** | Lower frequency but higher intent sessions; values speed and precision; more likely to pay for Premium immediately; uses version history and multiple resume management heavily. |
| **Technical Skills** | High digital literacy; may already use LinkedIn, Notion, and similar SaaS tools daily. |

### 3.4 Persona: The Recruiter (Future Expansion — Phase 3+)

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goals** | Quickly evaluate whether a candidate's resume matches a role; view standardized ATS/interview-readiness scores instead of raw resumes; source and shortlist candidates. |
| **Pain Points** | Manual resume screening at scale is slow; inconsistent resume formats make comparison hard; no visibility into a candidate's interview practice history. |
| **Usage Pattern** | Batch review sessions; searches/filters candidate pools; not a core MVP user — supported starting Phase 3. |
| **Technical Skills** | High; uses ATS/HRIS tools professionally. |

### 3.4a Persona Scenario Narratives

These short narratives illustrate how each core persona experiences the connected product loop described in Section 1.6, and should inform QA test scripts and onboarding design.

- **Ananya (Student):** Two weeks before her campus placement drive, Ananya signs up and, having no prior resume, works through the guided Profile Setup, translating a class project into three quantified bullet points with AI phrasing help. She picks an ATS-safe template, runs her first ATS scan against a sample job posting from her placement cell, and is surprised her score is only 54 — the scanner explains she is missing three core technical keywords the posting expects. She adds them, rescans, and reaches 78. She then starts a Resume-Based mock interview at "Entry" difficulty and is nervous about a question on a project gap; the feedback coaches her toward a STAR-format answer she reuses in her real interview a week later.
- **Rohan (Fresher):** Rohan is actively applying to 10+ roles a week. He maintains two resume variants (Data Analyst vs. Backend Engineer) and uses the ATS Scanner against nearly every posting before applying, applying suggested keyword insertions directly. He tracks every application in the Job Tracker, and when a card moves to "Interview," an automatic reminder fires 24 hours ahead, prompting him to run a JD-based mock interview session the night before. He exhausts his Free-tier monthly AI credits by week two and upgrades to Premium mid-cycle to keep pace with his search intensity.
- **Priya (Experienced Professional):** Priya is quietly exploring a pivot from individual-contributor PM to Group PM roles. She duplicates her existing resume, retailors the summary and top bullet points for leadership signals, and uses the Cover Letter Generator's "Formal" tone preset for a target company she's excited about. She values the version history feature heavily, since she iterates on wording across several sessions before feeling a version is "final" and locking it.

### 3.5 Persona: The Administrator ("Internal Operations/Support Staff")

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Goals** | Monitor platform health, manage subscriptions and refunds, moderate feedback/abuse, investigate AI usage anomalies, support user account issues. |
| **Pain Points** | Needs a single console instead of direct database access; needs audit trails for compliance; needs to act quickly on abuse or billing disputes. |
| **Usage Pattern** | Daily operational usage; dashboard-first; relies on search, filters, and exportable reports. |
| **Technical Skills** | High; comfortable with internal tools and structured data. |

### 4.1 Authentication (AUTH)

1. **AUTH-01** — As a new user, I want to sign up with my email and password so that I can create an account without needing a social login.
2. **AUTH-02** — As a new user, I want to sign up using my Google account so that I can register in one click without creating a new password.

6. **AUTH-06** — As a user resetting my password, I want the reset link to expire after a set time so that my account stays secure if the email is compromised later.
7. **AUTH-07** — As a logged-in user, I want my session to stay active across browser refreshes so that I don't have to log in repeatedly.
8. **AUTH-08** — As a security-conscious user, I want to log out of all devices at once so that I can secure my account if I suspect unauthorized access.
9. **AUTH-09** — As a user, I want to receive an email notification when a new device logs into my account so that I'm alerted to potential unauthorized access.

## 5. Functional Requirements

Each module below specifies detailed, testable requirements. Requirement IDs follow the pattern `FR-<MODULE>-<NUMBER>`.

### 5.1 Authentication Module

| ID | Requirement |
|---|---|
| FR-AUTH-01 | The system shall allow account creation using email and password. Email must be unique across the system. |
| FR-AUTH-02 | Passwords must be a minimum of 8 characters and require at least one uppercase letter, one lowercase letter, one number, and one special character. |
| FR-AUTH-03 | The system shall hash passwords using a strong adaptive hashing algorithm (e.g., bcrypt/Argon2) with per-user salt; plaintext passwords must never be stored or logged. |
| FR-AUTH-04 | The system shall support Google OAuth 2.0 sign-in and sign-up, linking to an existing account if the Google email matches an already-registered email. |
| FR-AUTH-05 | Upon email/password signup, the system shall send a verification email containing a time-limited (24-hour) verification link. |
| FR-AUTH-06 | Unverified accounts shall have restricted access (e.g., cannot export resumes or use AI features) until email verification is completed. |
| FR-AUTH-07 | The system shall allow a user to request a password reset via a "Forgot Password" flow, sending a time-limited (1-hour) reset link to the registered email. |
| FR-AUTH-08 | Password reset links shall be single-use and invalidated after use or expiry. |
| FR-AUTH-09 | The system shall issue a JWT access token (short-lived, e.g., 15 minutes) and a refresh token (long-lived, e.g., 7–30 days, rotated on use) upon successful login. |
| FR-AUTH-10 | The system shall maintain a session registry per user/device to support "logout from all devices," which invalidates all refresh tokens for that user. |
| FR-AUTH-11 | The system shall detect logins from a new device/browser fingerprint or unusual location and send a security notification email. |
| FR-AUTH-12 | The system shall allow a user to permanently delete their account, which triggers a confirmation step (re-authentication or typed confirmation) before irreversible deletion. |
| FR-AUTH-13 | Account deletion shall remove or anonymize personally identifiable data within a defined retention window (see Section 15, Data Privacy), while preserving anonymized analytics where legally permissible. |
| FR-AUTH-14 | The system shall rate-limit login attempts (e.g., lock or CAPTCHA-challenge after 5 failed attempts within 15 minutes) to mitigate brute-force attacks. |
| FR-AUTH-15 | The system shall support "Remember Me," extending refresh token lifetime when explicitly selected by the user. |
| FR-AUTH-16 | The system shall automatically expire idle sessions after a configurable inactivity window (default 30 days for refresh tokens; access tokens always short-lived). |

Profile pictures are used for account display only and are excluded from resume templates by default (with an explicit opt-in toggle for regions/industries where photos are customary). |

### 5.3 Resume Builder Module

| ID | Requirement |
|---|---|
| FR-RESB-01 | The system shall provide a minimum of 8 professionally designed resume templates at MVP launch, spanning at least 3 style categories (Modern, Classic/ATS-safe, Creative). |
| FR-RESB-02 | Each template shall be clearly labeled with an "ATS Safety" indicator (e.g., "ATS-Optimized," "Visually Rich — lower ATS compatibility") to set user expectations. |
| FR-RESB-03 | The system shall provide a section-based drag-and-drop editor allowing reordering of standard sections (Summary, Experience, Education, Skills, Projects, Certifications, etc.). |
| FR-RESB-04 | The system shall render a live, WYSIWYG preview reflecting edits in real time (target: visual update within 300ms of keystroke, debounced). |
| FR-RESB-05 | The system shall allow creation of custom sections with a user-defined title and free-form bullet content (e.g., "Publications," "Awards," "Volunteer Experience"). |
| FR-RESB-06 | The system shall auto-save resume content at minimum every 10 seconds of inactivity and on every field blur event, with a visible "Saved" / "Saving..." indicator. |
| FR-RESB-07 | The system shall allow a user to duplicate any existing resume, creating an independent copy with "(Copy)" appended to the title. |
| FR-RESB-08 | The system shall export resumes to PDF, preserving template layout, fonts, and formatting, with text remaining selectable/copyable (not rasterized) to remain ATS-parseable. |
| FR-RESB-09 | The system shall allow theme customization: accent color, font family (from a curated ATS-safe font list), and section spacing, without breaking template layout integrity. |
| FR-RESB-10 | The system shall retain version history for each resume (minimum last 20 versions or 90 days, whichever is greater for Premium; last 5 versions for Free), allowing preview and one-click restore of a prior version. |
| FR-RESB-11 | The system shall support unlimited resume renaming and organizing into a resume list view with last-edited timestamps. |
| FR-RESB-12 | The system shall support resume deletion with a confirmation prompt and a 30-day "trash" recovery window before permanent deletion. |
| FR-RESB-13 | The system shall display a real-time page-length indicator and warn when content exceeds one page (configurable target length). |
| FR-RESB-14 | The system shall run inline spelling/grammar checks on resume text fields and visually flag issues with suggested corrections. |
| FR-RESB-15 | The system shall offer AI-assisted bullet-point rewriting suggestions (e.g., strengthening action verbs, adding quantifiable metrics) inline within the editor. |
| FR-RESB-16 | The system shall allow a resume to be marked "Final/Locked," disabling further edits until explicitly unlocked, to prevent accidental changes to a submitted version. |
| FR-RESB-17 | The resume builder shall be fully functional on mobile viewports (responsive editing UI), acknowledging that complex layout editing may be simplified on small screens. |
| FR-RESB-18 | The system shall allow generation of a shareable, read-only public link to a resume (view-only, no account required), revocable at any time by the owner. |
| FR-RESB-19 | The system shall enforce a per-plan limit on the number of active resumes (e.g., Free: 2, Premium: unlimited). |

### 5.4 ATS Scanner Module

| ID | Requirement |
|---|---|
| FR-ATS-01 | The system shall allow a user to select one of their existing resumes and paste or upload a job description for analysis. |
| FR-ATS-02 | The system shall generate an overall ATS Compatibility Score (0–100) combining keyword match, formatting compliance, and structural completeness sub-scores. |
| FR-ATS-03 | The system shall display a breakdown of sub-scores (e.g., Keyword Match: 65/100, Formatting: 90/100, Structure: 85/100). |
| FR-ATS-04 | The system shall extract and display keywords present in the job description but missing from the resume, ranked by relevance/frequency. |
| FR-ATS-05 | The system shall detect formatting risks including: tables, text boxes, headers/footers containing critical info, embedded images with text, non-standard fonts, and multi-column layouts, and shall flag each with a plain-language explanation. |
| FR-ATS-06 | The system shall provide a written explanation for each weakness identified (not just a flag), including a specific suggested fix. |
| FR-ATS-07 | The system shall allow the user to apply a suggested keyword insertion directly into the relevant resume section with one click, subject to user confirmation. |
| FR-ATS-08 | The system shall store a history of all scans per resume, including score, job description snippet/title, and timestamp, viewable in a trend chart. |
| FR-ATS-09 | The system shall allow comparison of two resume versions (or two resumes) against the same job description side-by-side. |
| FR-ATS-10 | Each ATS scan shall consume a defined number of AI credits, deducted only upon successful scan completion (not on failure). |
| FR-ATS-11 | The system shall cap job description input length (e.g., 10,000 characters) and reject/prompt truncation beyond that limit. |

| FR-COVER-06 | The system shall allow saving multiple named cover letters per user, associable with specific Job Tracker entries. |
| FR-COVER-07 | Generated content shall avoid fabricating experience or credentials not present in the user's profile/resume data; the AI prompt layer shall be constrained to only elaborate on provided facts. |
| FR-COVER-08 | Each cover letter generation/regeneration shall consume a defined number of AI credits. |

### 5.6 AI Interview Module

| ID | Requirement |
|---|---|
| FR-INT-01 | The system shall support five interview types: HR/General, Technical, Behavioral, Resume-Based, and Job-Description-Based. |
| FR-INT-02 | For Resume-Based interviews, the system shall generate questions derived from the user's selected resume content (specific projects, roles, gaps). |
| FR-INT-03 | For Job-Description-Based interviews, the system shall generate questions derived from a pasted job description, weighted toward required skills/qualifications. |
| FR-INT-04 | The system shall allow the user to select difficulty level (Entry, Mid, Senior) and approximate session length (Quick: 5 questions, Standard: 10 questions, Deep: 15+ questions). |
| FR-INT-05 | The system shall accept answers via typed text input and, where browser support allows, via voice input transcribed to text. |
| FR-INT-06 | The system shall score each answer on a defined rubric (e.g., Relevance, Structure/Clarity, Specificity/Evidence, Confidence-of-language) producing a per-answer score (0–10) and an aggregate session score. |
| FR-INT-07 | The system shall provide specific, actionable improvement feedback per answer (not generic praise/criticism), referencing the actual content of the user's answer. |
| FR-INT-08 | The system shall allow the user to pause a session and resume later without losing progress, for up to 24 hours before the draft session expires. |
| FR-INT-09 | The system shall generate a full session transcript (question + answer + score + feedback) accessible after completion. |
| FR-INT-10 | The system shall allow retrying an individual question within an active or completed session, storing the retry as a new attempt without overwriting the original for progress-tracking purposes. |
| FR-INT-11 | The system shall track and visualize interview performance trends over time (score by date, score by interview type). |
| FR-INT-12 | The system shall produce an end-of-session summary identifying top 2–3 strengths and top 2–3 areas for improvement. |
| FR-INT-13 | Each interview question generation and each answer evaluation shall consume defined AI credits, with the cost model disclosed to the user before starting a session. |
| FR-INT-14 | The system shall impose a maximum answer length (e.g., 3,000 characters or 5 minutes of voice) per question to control processing cost and abuse. |

## 6. Non-Functional Requirements

| Category | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Performance** | P95 page load time ≤ 2 seconds on broadband; P95 AI feature response time ≤ 8 seconds (with streaming/progressive UI where possible); PDF export generation ≤ 3 seconds for a standard 1–2 page resume. |
| **Scalability** | The system shall be architected to scale horizontally to support at least 100,000 registered users and 10,000 concurrent sessions at launch scale, with a clear path to 1M+ users via stateless application servers and managed autoscaling. |
| **Availability** | Target uptime SLA of 99.9% (excluding scheduled maintenance windows, communicated 48 hours in advance). Third-party AI provider outages shall degrade gracefully (queued retry / clear user messaging) rather than crash the app. |
| **Accessibility** | The platform shall conform to WCAG 2.1 Level AA, including keyboard navigability, screen-reader-compatible labeling (ARIA), sufficient color contrast (≥ 4.5:1 for body text), and resizable text without loss of functionality. |
| **Security** | See Section 15 in full. Summary: encrypted data at rest and in transit, secure authentication, input validation, dependency vulnerability scanning, regular penetration testing. |
| **Privacy** | The system shall comply with applicable data protection regulations (e.g., GDPR, CCPA) including right to access, right to erasure, and data portability (resume/profile export in machine-readable format). |
| **Reliability** | Automated backups of the primary database at minimum daily, with point-in-time recovery capability; Recovery Point Objective (RPO) ≤ 24 hours, Recovery Time Objective (RTO) ≤ 4 hours. |
| **Maintainability** | Codebase shall follow documented style guides, maintain automated test coverage of at least 70% for core business logic, and use CI/CD pipelines with mandatory code review before merge to main. |
| **Responsiveness** | The UI shall be fully responsive across breakpoints for mobile (≥360px), tablet, and desktop, with no horizontal scrolling or broken layouts at supported breakpoints. |
| **SEO** | Public-facing marketing pages (landing, pricing, blog) shall support server-side rendering or static generation, semantic HTML, meta tags, sitemap.xml, and robots.txt. Authenticated app pages are excluded from indexing (noindex). |
| **Logging** | The system shall log all API requests (method, endpoint, status, latency, user ID where authenticated) with PII redaction in log payloads; logs retained for a minimum of 90 days. |
| **Monitoring** | The system shall implement application performance monitoring (APM), uptime monitoring, and error tracking with alerting thresholds (e.g., error rate > 2% over 5 minutes triggers on-call alert). |
| **Localization Readiness** | Although MVP ships in English only, all user-facing strings shall be externalized (not hardcoded) to enable future localization without major refactoring. |
| **Browser Support** | The platform shall support the latest two major versions of Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge. |
| **Data Portability** | Users shall be able to export their full profile and resume data in a structured, machine-readable format (JSON) on request. |

### 8.2 Primary Navigation Flow

```
Landing Page → Sign Up → Email Verification → Onboarding (Profile Setup)
      → Dashboard (hub)
           ├── Resume Builder → Live Editor → Export PDF
           ├── ATS Scanner → Score Report → Apply Suggestions → back to Editor
           ├── Cover Letter Generator → Editor → Export
           ├── AI Interview → Session Setup → Live Session → Summary/Transcript
           ├── Job Tracker → Kanban Board → Job Detail
           └── Billing/Upgrade → Checkout → Confirmation
```

### 8.3 Key User Journeys

**Journey 1 — First-Time Resume Creation**
Signup → Email Verification → Guided Profile Setup (or Import Resume) → Choose Template → Resume Builder (auto-save) → Live Preview → Export PDF → Prompt: "Run an ATS Scan on this resume?"

**Journey 2 — Job-Specific Tailoring Loop**
Dashboard → ATS Scanner → Select Resume + Paste JD → View Score & Missing Keywords → Apply Suggestions → Re-scan → Generate Cover Letter (reuses JD) → Add Job to Tracker (auto-links resume/cover letter) → Set Application Deadline

**Journey 3 — Interview Preparation Loop**
Dashboard → AI Interview → Select Type (e.g., Job-Description-Based) + Paste JD → Session Setup (difficulty, length) → Answer Questions → Per-Answer Feedback → Session Summary → Review Transcript → (Reminder scheduled if linked Job Tracker entry has upcoming interview)

**Journey 4 — Upgrade Flow**
Free user hits AI credit limit → In-context upgrade prompt → Pricing Comparison Modal → Checkout (Stripe) → Confirmation → Immediate unlock of Premium limits → Billing History updated

**Journey 5 — Admin Support Resolution**
Admin receives escalated support ticket (external system) → `/admin/users` search by email → View account/subscription/AI usage → Issue credit adjustment or refund with reason code → Action logged in Audit Log → Optional user notification sent

## 10. Edge Cases

Organized by domain. Each includes the expected system behavior.

### 10.1 Account & Authentication

1. **Duplicate signup attempt** with an already-registered email → system rejects with "Account already exists," offers login/reset link instead.
2. **Google OAuth email matches existing email/password account** → system links accounts (with user confirmation) rather than creating a duplicate.
3. **Expired email verification token** → system shows expiry message with option to resend a new verification email.
4. **Expired password reset token** → system rejects the reset attempt and prompts the user to request a new link.
5. **Password reset link used twice** → second use is rejected as invalid/already-used.
6. **User deletes account then tries to sign up again with the same email immediately** → system allows re-registration as a new account (previous data anonymized/purged per retention policy), or if within grace/undo period, offers account restoration instead.
7. **JWT access token expires mid-session** → client silently refreshes using refresh token; if refresh token also invalid/expired, user is redirected to login without losing unsaved resume data (recovered from local auto-save cache).
8. **User logs out from all devices while actively editing on another device** → the other device's next API call fails with 401; client detects this, preserves unsaved edits locally, and prompts re-login.
9. **Concurrent login attempts from many devices** → all succeed unless "logout other sessions" is explicitly triggered; session registry tracks each independently.
10. **User's email provider blocks/bounces verification email** → system flags delivery failure in logs and offers an alternate "verify via support" path after repeated bounce.
11. **Malicious repeated failed login attempts (brute force)** → rate limiting/CAPTCHA triggers after threshold; account is not locked outright to avoid denial-of-service via forced lockouts, but requires CAPTCHA/delay.

### 10.2 Profile & Resume Data

12. **User uploads a resume for import that is a scanned image (non-text PDF)** → OCR fallback attempted; if parsing confidence is low, system informs user and requests manual entry rather than silently producing garbage data.
13. **User enters an end date earlier than the start date for an experience/education entry** → inline validation error blocks save until corrected.
14. **User leaves all resume sections empty and attempts PDF export** → export is blocked with a message indicating minimum required sections (e.g., name and one content section).
15. **Extremely long text entered in a bullet point (e.g., 5,000 characters)** → input is capped with a visible character counter and warning before the cap.
16. **User pastes rich-formatted text (from Word) into a plain text field** → system sanitizes/strips unsupported formatting while preserving line breaks.
17. **Special characters or emoji in name/company fields** → accepted and rendered correctly in both UI and PDF export (Unicode-safe).
18. **User uploads a profile picture that is not a valid image file** → upload rejected with clear file-type error before any processing occurs.
19. **User uploads an oversized profile picture (>5MB)** → rejected with size-limit message; client-side pre-validation prevents unnecessary upload attempt.

### 10.3 Resume Builder

20. **Two browser tabs editing the same resume simultaneously (concurrent edits)** → last-write-wins with a warning banner if the system detects the resume was modified elsewhere since the tab loaded; user is offered to reload latest version before continuing.
21. **Auto-save fails due to network interruption mid-edit** → client retries with exponential backoff, queues changes locally (e.g., in browser storage/state), and clearly indicates "Not saved — retrying" until success.
22. **User's content overflows the one-page target in a strict ATS template** → system displays a soft warning (not a hard block) with a suggestion to trim, since some users intentionally need 2 pages (e.g., senior professionals).
23. **User switches templates after heavy customization** → system attempts best-effort content remapping into the new template's sections; any content that doesn't map cleanly (e.g., custom sections unsupported by target template) is flagged rather than silently dropped.
24. **PDF export requested while resume has unsaved changes** → system forces a save (or saves the current in-memory state) before generating the export, ensuring exported PDF always matches what the user sees.
25. **PDF export fails due to server-side rendering error** → user sees a clear failure message with a retry option; the failure is logged with resume ID for engineering triage; AI/export credit (if applicable) is not deducted on failure.
26. **User attempts to restore a version from history that no longer matches current section schema (e.g., template deprecated)** → system performs a compatibility check and either maps content forward or warns that certain elements can't be restored exactly.
27. **User exceeds the plan's active resume limit and attempts to create another** → creation is blocked with an upgrade prompt; existing resumes remain fully accessible/editable/exportable.
28. **User deletes a resume that is linked to an active Job Tracker entry** → system warns that the resume is referenced by N active applications before allowing deletion, and preserves a locked snapshot reference in the Job Tracker entry regardless.

### 10.4 ATS Scanner & AI Features

29. **AI service (LLM provider) times out or returns an error** → user sees a friendly "We couldn't complete this right now, please try again" message; the action is retried automatically once server-side before surfacing the error; AI credit is not deducted on failure.
30. **AI service returns malformed/unparseable output** → server-side validation catches the malformed response and triggers a single automatic retry before failing gracefully to the user.
31. **User pastes a job description containing no meaningful text (e.g., empty, gibberish, or non-job content)** → system detects low-confidence input and prompts the user to verify/re-paste rather than generating a nonsensical score.
32. **User pastes an extremely long job description (>10,000 characters)** → input is truncated at the limit with a visible notice, and analysis proceeds on the truncated content.
33. **User runs the same ATS scan twice in rapid succession** → system may serve a cached result within a short window (e.g., 60 seconds) to avoid double-charging AI credits for an identical, accidental duplicate request.
34. **Resume contains no parseable text (e.g., entirely image-based PDF was somehow uploaded)** → scanner detects zero extractable text and returns a specific "Unreadable resume format" result instead of a misleading low score.
35. **User runs out of AI credits mid-multi-step flow (e.g., mid cover-letter regeneration)** → the in-progress single request that was already initiated completes normally (already authorized), but the next action is blocked with an upgrade/credit prompt.

### 10.5 AI Interview

36. **User's internet disconnects mid-interview session** → in-progress answer is preserved in local state; upon reconnect, the session resumes from the last unanswered/in-progress question rather than restarting.
37. **User provides an extremely short or blank answer (e.g., "idk")** → system still scores it (low score) and provides constructive feedback rather than erroring out.
38. **Voice transcription produces low-confidence or garbled text** → system flags low transcription confidence and allows the user to manually edit the transcribed text before submission.
39. **User closes the browser tab mid-session without pausing** → session is treated as auto-paused (via periodic state sync) and is resumable within the pause expiry window (24 hours) rather than lost.
40. **User attempts to start a new interview session while one is already "in progress"** → system prompts to resume the existing session or explicitly abandon it before starting a new one.
41. **Generated question is inappropriate, irrelevant, or nonsensical for the given resume/JD** → user can flag the question; flagged questions are excluded from that session's scoring impact and logged for AI quality review.

### 10.6 Job Tracker

42. **User creates a job entry with a deadline date in the past** → system accepts it (for logging past applications) but does not generate a future reminder for it.
43. **User drags a job card directly from "Wishlist" to "Offer," skipping intermediate stages** → allowed; the system does not enforce a strict linear stage progression, since real-world processes vary.
44. **Duplicate job entry for the same company/role added twice** → system does not block duplicates outright (user may be tracking multiple postings) but surfaces a soft warning ("Similar entry already exists") to prevent accidental duplication.
45. **Linked resume/cover letter for a job entry is later deleted** → the Job Tracker entry retains a locked historical snapshot/reference so context isn't lost, and displays "Original document deleted — showing archived snapshot."

### 10.7 Subscription & Billing

46. **Payment fails on subscription renewal** → system retries payment per a defined dunning schedule (e.g., day 1, 3, 7), notifies the user each attempt, and only downgrades access after the grace period fully lapses.
47. **User applies an expired or invalid coupon code** → checkout rejects the code with a specific reason (expired, invalid, usage limit reached) rather than a generic error.
48. **User attempts to apply a coupon a second time after already using it (single-use coupon)** → rejected with "Coupon already used" message.
49. **User downgrades from Premium to Free mid-billing-cycle** → Premium access continues until the end of the paid period; downgrade takes effect at the next billing date, clearly communicated at cancellation time.
50. **Currency/region mismatch (user's card currency differs from plan pricing currency)** → payment processor handles conversion; system displays the charged amount in the processor's confirmed currency post-transaction.
51. **Chargeback/dispute filed by user's bank** → account is flagged for admin review and access may be suspended pending resolution, per configured dispute-handling policy.
52. **User's AI credits don't fully reset due to a billing cycle date edge case (e.g., subscribed on Jan 31, next cycle in a 28-day month)** → system normalizes billing anchor dates to avoid skipped or duplicate cycles (e.g., anchor to last valid day of month).

### 10.8 File Uploads & Exports

53. **User uploads a corrupted or password-protected PDF for import** → system detects the failure mode specifically and informs the user (e.g., "This PDF is password-protected; please remove the password and re-upload") rather than a generic error.
54. **User uploads a file with an incorrect extension (e.g., .docx renamed to .pdf)** → system validates actual file content/MIME type, not just the extension, and rejects mismatches with a clear message.
55. **Export requested during a temporary PDF-rendering service outage** → system queues the request or clearly communicates a temporary service degradation, without charging credits or silently failing.

### 10.9 System & Platform

56. **User is on the Job Tracker board when their subscription simultaneously lapses (e.g., failed payment resolved mid-session) mid-session in another tab** → system re-checks entitlement on next relevant action and gracefully restricts new premium actions without disrupting the already-loaded read view.
57. **Admin edits a user's subscription tier while the user is actively mid-checkout for a different plan** → system uses optimistic locking/latest-state checks at the payment confirmation step to prevent conflicting states.
58. **Rate limit exceeded on a public API endpoint (e.g., scripted abuse of the shared resume link)** → requests beyond the limit receive a 429 response with a clear retry-after header, without crashing the underlying service.
59. **Clock skew between client and server affects "deadline" or "reminder" accuracy** → all time-sensitive logic is computed server-side in UTC, with client-side display only, to avoid skew-related bugs.
60. **User's browser has JavaScript disabled or is using an unsupported legacy browser** → system displays a graceful fallback message indicating minimum browser requirements rather than a blank/broken page.

## 11. Error Handling

### 11.1 General Principles

- Every user-facing error shall include: a plain-language explanation, a suggested next action, and (where relevant) a support/retry option.
- No raw stack traces, database errors, or internal identifiers shall be exposed to end users; internal details are logged server-side with a correlation/request ID surfaced to the user for support reference.
- AI credits and paid actions shall never be deducted for a failed/incomplete operation.
- Destructive actions (delete account, delete resume, cancel subscription) always require explicit confirmation.

### 11.2 Error Handling by Category

| Error Type | System Behavior |
|---|---|
| **Validation errors** (bad input format) | Inline, field-level error messages shown immediately on blur/submit; form submission blocked until resolved. |
| **Authentication errors** (401) | Redirect to login with a "session expired" message; unsaved work preserved locally where feasible. |
| **Authorization errors** (403) | Generic "You don't have permission to do this" message; no leakage of whether the resource exists. |
| **Not found errors** (404) | Friendly "This item couldn't be found — it may have been deleted" page/message with navigation back to a safe page. |
| **Rate limit errors** (429) | "You're doing that a bit too fast — please try again in [X] seconds" with a visible countdown where practical. |
| **AI provider errors / timeouts** (5xx from AI layer) | One automatic retry server-side; if still failing, user sees "AI service is temporarily unavailable, please try again shortly," credit not deducted. |
| **Payment errors** | Specific decline reason shown when available from processor (e.g., "insufficient funds," "card expired"); generic message otherwise; user prompted to update payment method. |
| **File upload errors** | Specific reason (size, type, corruption) shown pre- or immediately post-upload attempt. |
| **Network/connectivity errors** | Client detects offline state, queues safe actions (e.g., resume edits) locally, and displays a persistent "You're offline — changes will sync when reconnected" banner. |
| **Server errors** (500, unexpected) | Generic "Something went wrong on our end" message with a correlation ID; automatically logged and alerted to on-call engineering above defined error-rate thresholds. |
| **Concurrent edit conflicts** | Non-destructive conflict banner offering "Reload latest version" vs. "Keep my changes," never silently overwriting. |
| **Partial batch failures** (e.g., bulk export) | Report per-item success/failure rather than an all-or-nothing failure; user can retry only the failed items. |

### 11.3 Retry & Idempotency

- All AI-generation requests shall carry an idempotency key so that client-side retries (e.g., due to a flaky network response) do not result in duplicate credit deduction or duplicate content generation.
- Payment operations shall be idempotent at the payment-processor integration layer to prevent double-charging on retry.

## 12. Acceptance Criteria

Acceptance criteria are expressed in Given/When/Then form for representative core flows per module. Engineering/QA shall expand these into full test cases per user story in Section 4.

### 12.1 Authentication

- **Given** a new user on the signup page, **when** they submit a valid email and a password meeting complexity rules, **then** an account is created in an "unverified" state and a verification email is sent within 60 seconds.
- **Given** an unverified account, **when** the user clicks a valid, unexpired verification link, **then** the account status changes to "verified" and the user is redirected to onboarding.
- **Given** a verified user, **when** they enter correct credentials on login, **then** they are authenticated and redirected to `/dashboard` within 2 seconds.
- **Given** a user requests a password reset, **when** they submit a new password via a valid reset link, **then** the old password no longer works and the new password authenticates successfully.

### 12.2 Resume Builder

- **Given** a user on the resume editor, **when** they type in any text field, **then** the change is auto-saved within 10 seconds and the save indicator updates to "Saved."
- **Given** a completed resume, **when** the user clicks "Export PDF," **then** a PDF downloads within 3 seconds containing selectable (non-rasterized) text matching the live preview exactly.
- **Given** a resume with 25 saved versions, **when** the user opens Version History, **then** they can preview and restore any of the most recent 20 (Premium) or 5 (Free) versions.

### 12.3 ATS Scanner

- **Given** a selected resume and a pasted job description, **when** the user clicks "Scan," **then** an overall score (0–100) and at least one missing-keyword suggestion are returned within 30 seconds, assuming a non-empty, valid job description.
- **Given** a resume with a table-based layout, **when** scanned, **then** the formatting sub-score reflects a penalty and a specific formatting warning is displayed referencing the table usage.

### 12.4 Cover Letter Generator

- **Given** a selected resume, job description, company name, and tone, **when** the user clicks "Generate," **then** a complete, properly formatted cover letter (salutation through closing) is produced within 20 seconds.
- **Given** a generated cover letter, **when** the user clicks "Regenerate," **then** a materially different draft is produced using the same inputs, and one additional AI credit is deducted only upon successful generation.

### 12.5 AI Interview

- **Given** a user starts a Resume-Based interview at "Mid" difficulty with 10 questions, **when** the session begins, **then** 10 questions are generated referencing specific resume content within 15 seconds of session start.
- **Given** a submitted answer, **when** evaluation completes, **then** the user receives a numeric score and at least one specific, non-generic improvement suggestion referencing the submitted answer's content.
- **Given** an interview session paused for under 24 hours, **when** the user returns, **then** the session resumes exactly at the next unanswered question with all prior answers/scores intact.

### 12.6 Job Tracker

- **Given** a job entry in "Applied," **when** the user drags it to "Interview," **then** the stage updates immediately, the transition timestamp is recorded, and the change is reflected in pipeline statistics without a page reload.
- **Given** a job entry with a deadline set for tomorrow, **when** the reminder job runs, **then** the user receives a notification (per their channel preference) at least 24 hours before the deadline.

### 12.7 Subscription & Billing

- **Given** a Free-plan user who has used all monthly ATS scans, **when** they attempt another scan, **then** the scan is blocked and an upgrade prompt is shown instead of a partial/degraded result.
- **Given** a user completing checkout for Premium, **when** payment succeeds, **then** their plan tier and AI credit limits update within 5 seconds and are reflected immediately on the Dashboard and Billing pages.

### 12.8 Admin

- **Given** an admin viewing a user's account, **when** they issue a manual credit adjustment with a reason code, **then** the user's credit balance updates immediately and an audit log entry is created capturing admin ID, user ID, old value, new value, reason, and timestamp.

## 14. Database Requirements

*(Described conceptually — no SQL/DDL provided, per instructions. Engineering may implement in a relational or hybrid document/relational model as appropriate.)*

### 14.1 Entity List

1. **User** — core account record (email, password hash, auth provider, email verification status, role, account status, timestamps).
2. **UserProfile** — personal details, summary/headline, profile picture reference.
3. **Education** — belongs to User; institution, degree, dates, GPA.
4. **Experience** — belongs to User; company, title, dates, bullet points.
5. **Skill** — belongs to User; name, category, proficiency (optional).
6. **Language** — belongs to User; name, proficiency level.
7. **SocialLink** — belongs to User; platform, URL.
8. **Resume** — belongs to User; title, templateId, theme settings, content (structured sections), status (draft/final), lock state.
9. **ResumeSection** — belongs to Resume; type (standard/custom), order, title, content blocks.
10. **ResumeVersion** — belongs to Resume; snapshot of content, timestamp, change summary.
11. **Template** — design metadata; name, category, ATS-safety rating, preview asset reference.
12. **AtsScan** — belongs to User and Resume; job description snippet/hash, overall score, sub-scores, missing keywords, formatting issues, timestamp.
13. **CoverLetter** — belongs to User; title, content, tone, associated resumeId, associated jobId (optional).
14. **InterviewSession** — belongs to User; type, difficulty, status (in-progress/paused/completed), source resumeId or job description reference, start/end timestamps.
15. **InterviewQuestion** — belongs to InterviewSession; question text, order, category.
16. **InterviewAnswer** — belongs to InterviewQuestion; answer text, score, rubric breakdown, feedback text, attempt number, timestamp.
17. **JobEntry** — belongs to User; company, role, stage, source, notes, tags, linked resumeVersionId, linked coverLetterId, dates (applied, deadlines, interview dates).
18. **JobStageHistory** — belongs to JobEntry; stage, transitioned-at timestamp.
19. **Notification** — belongs to User; type, content, read status, channel, created-at.
20. **NotificationPreference** — belongs to User; category-level channel opt-in/opt-out settings.
21. **Subscription** — belongs to User; plan tier, billing cycle, status, renewal date, payment processor customer/subscription ID.
22. **Invoice** — belongs to Subscription; amount, currency, status, date, downloadable receipt reference.
23. **Coupon** — code, discount type/value, expiry, usage limits, redemption count.
24. **AiCreditLedger** — belongs to User; transaction type (grant/consume/refund), amount, related feature/action, balance-after, timestamp.
25. **AuditLog** — actor (admin) ID, action type, target entity/ID, before/after values, reason code, timestamp.
26. **FeedbackTicket** — belongs to User; category, message, status, admin assignee, resolution notes.
27. **Session/RefreshToken** — belongs to User; device/browser metadata, issued-at, expires-at, revoked flag.
28. **AdminUser** — extends User with admin role scope (e.g., Support Admin, Super Admin — Phase 3+).

### 14.2 Key Relationships

- **User 1—1 UserProfile**
- **User 1—N** Education, Experience, Skill, Language, SocialLink, Resume, CoverLetter, InterviewSession, JobEntry, Notification, AtsScan, AiCreditLedger entries, FeedbackTicket, Session/RefreshToken
- **Resume 1—N ResumeSection, ResumeVersion, AtsScan**
- **Template 1—N Resume** (a template is referenced by many resumes)
- **InterviewSession 1—N InterviewQuestion**
- **InterviewQuestion 1—N InterviewAnswer** (multiple attempts per question)
- **JobEntry 1—N JobStageHistory**
- **JobEntry N—1 Resume (via ResumeVersion snapshot), N—1 CoverLetter** (optional linkage)
- **User 1—1 Subscription; Subscription 1—N Invoice**
- **Coupon N—N User** (via redemption records, tracking per-user usage limits)
- **AdminUser 1—N AuditLog entries** (as actor)

### 14.3 Business Rule Constraints

- A `User.email` must be unique across the system regardless of auth provider (email/password vs. Google).
- A `Resume` must belong to exactly one `User`; deleting a `User` cascades to a soft-delete/anonymization of owned resumes per data retention policy, not an immediate hard delete.
- `ResumeVersion` records are immutable once created (append-only) to guarantee reliable history/restore.
- `JobEntry.linkedResumeVersionId` must reference a snapshot, not a live/mutable resume, to preserve "what was actually submitted" integrity even if the source resume later changes.
- `AiCreditLedger` balance must never go negative; any action requiring credits must check available balance before executing, within a single atomic transaction/check to prevent race-condition overspend.
- `Coupon` redemption must enforce both global usage limit and per-user usage limit atomically to prevent race-condition double-redemption.
- `AuditLog` records are append-only and must never be updated or deleted, including by Super Admin, to preserve integrity for compliance investigations.
- `InterviewAnswer` retries must be stored as new rows referencing an incrementing `attemptNumber`, never overwriting a prior attempt, to preserve accurate progress-tracking history.

### 14.4 Index Recommendations

- `User.email` — unique index (login lookups, duplicate prevention).
- `Resume.userId`, `CoverLetter.userId`, `JobEntry.userId`, `InterviewSession.userId` — foreign-key indexes for fast "list my X" queries.
- `AtsScan.resumeId` + `AtsScan.createdAt` — composite index for scan-history trend queries.
- `JobEntry.userId` + `JobEntry.stage` — composite index for Kanban board queries.
- `Notification.userId` + `Notification.read` — composite index for unread-count queries.
- `AiCreditLedger.userId` + `AiCreditLedger.createdAt` — composite index for balance calculation and usage-history queries.
- `AuditLog.targetEntityId`, `AuditLog.actorId`, `AuditLog.createdAt` — indexes for investigation queries.
- `Session/RefreshToken.userId` + `revoked` — composite index for fast "logout all devices" invalidation queries.
- `Coupon.code` — unique index for checkout lookup.

## 15. Security Requirements

| Category | Requirement |
|---|---|
| **Authentication** | Passwords hashed with a modern adaptive algorithm (bcrypt/Argon2, cost factor tuned per current guidance); JWT access tokens short-lived (≤15 min); refresh tokens rotated on each use and revocable individually or in bulk. |
| **Authorization** | All API endpoints enforce role-based access control (RBAC) server-side (Guest/Registered/Premium/Admin); client-side checks are UX-only and never trusted as the security boundary. Object-level authorization (e.g., a user can only access their own resumes) enforced on every request. |
| **Password Hashing** | Never store or log plaintext passwords; hashing performed server-side immediately on receipt over TLS. |
| **CSRF** | State-changing requests protected via anti-CSRF tokens or SameSite cookie policies combined with header-based auth tokens (not relying solely on cookies for session state). |
| **XSS** | All user-generated content (resume text, cover letter edits, job notes) is output-encoded/escaped on render; rich-text inputs sanitized against a strict allow-list of formatting tags server-side before storage. |
| **SQL Injection** | All database access via parameterized queries/ORM; no raw string concatenation of user input into queries. |
| **Rate Limiting** | Applied per-IP and per-user on authentication endpoints, AI-generation endpoints, and public share-link endpoints, with sensible defaults (e.g., login: 5/15min; AI actions: governed primarily by credit system but also floor-limited against burst abuse). |
| **API Abuse Prevention** | AI endpoints require valid authentication and sufficient credit balance; anomalous usage patterns (e.g., thousands of scans/hour from one account) trigger automated flags for admin review and temporary throttling. |
| **Secure File Uploads** | Uploaded files (resumes, profile pictures) validated by content/MIME type (not extension alone), size-capped, scanned for malware where feasible, and stored in isolated object storage (not directly executable/servable paths). |
| **Data Encryption** | All data encrypted in transit via TLS 1.2+; sensitive data at rest (passwords, tokens, payment references) encrypted using industry-standard encryption; database-level encryption at rest enabled. |
| **Audit Logging** | All admin actions and sensitive user actions (account deletion, subscription changes, security setting changes) are logged immutably with actor, action, target, and timestamp (see Section 14.3). |
| **Secrets Management** | API keys, database credentials, and third-party secrets stored in a dedicated secrets manager, never committed to source control or exposed to client-side code. |
| **Third-Party AI Data Handling** | User data sent to AI providers for processing (resume content, job descriptions, interview answers) is limited to what's necessary for the specific request; contractual/data-processing agreements with AI providers ensure no unauthorized retention or training use of user data without consent. |
| **Dependency Security** | Automated dependency vulnerability scanning integrated into CI/CD; critical vulnerabilities block deployment until patched or explicitly risk-accepted. |
| **Penetration Testing** | Third-party security assessment/penetration test conducted at minimum annually and before major architecture changes (e.g., introducing recruiter-facing data sharing). |
| **MFA for Admin** | Admin Dashboard access requires multi-factor authentication in addition to standard credentials. |
| **PII Minimization** | The system collects only the personal data necessary to deliver the product; optional fields (e.g., photo) are clearly marked as such and excluded from default resume exports. |

## 16. UX Requirements

### 16.1 Design Principles

1. **Clarity over cleverness** — every AI suggestion must be explainable in plain language; no black-box scores without justification.
2. **Progress visibility** — users should always be able to see where they stand (profile completion, resume score, application pipeline, interview readiness).
3. **Low-friction entry** — a first-time user should be able to produce a usable resume without reading documentation.
4. **Trust through transparency** — AI credit costs, plan limits, and data usage are always visible before an action is taken, never hidden until after the fact.
5. **Consistency** — shared design tokens (color, spacing, typography) and interaction patterns (buttons, modals, toasts) applied uniformly across all modules.

### 16.2 Responsive Behavior

- Layouts adapt fluidly across breakpoints (mobile ≥360px, tablet ≥768px, desktop ≥1024px, wide ≥1440px).
- Complex editing surfaces (Resume Builder drag-and-drop, Job Tracker Kanban) provide a simplified, touch-friendly interaction mode on mobile (e.g., reorder via up/down controls instead of drag where drag is unreliable on touch).
- Tables and dense data views (billing history, admin analytics) become horizontally scrollable or card-based on narrow viewports rather than breaking layout.

### 16.3 Accessibility

- All interactive elements reachable and operable via keyboard alone (tab order logical, focus states visible).
- All meaningful images/icons carry appropriate `alt` text or `aria-label`; decorative elements marked `aria-hidden`.
- Color is never the sole means of conveying information (e.g., ATS score risk levels paired with text labels, not just red/green).
- Form errors are announced to screen readers via `aria-live` regions.
- Minimum touch target size of 44x44px on interactive mobile elements.

### 16.4 Loading States

- Skeleton loaders (not blank screens or spinners alone) for content-heavy views (dashboard, resume list, job tracker board) to reduce perceived latency.
- AI-generation actions display a progress indicator with contextual messaging (e.g., "Analyzing your resume against the job description...") rather than a generic spinner, and streamed/partial results where the underlying AI response supports streaming.
- Actions expected to take >3 seconds display an estimated wait or a cancel option.

### 16.5 Empty States

- Every list/board view (resumes, job tracker, cover letters, interview history, notifications) has a designed empty state with a clear call-to-action (e.g., empty Job Tracker → "Add your first job to start tracking your search").
- Empty states are encouraging and instructional, not just blank placeholders.

### 16.6 Error States

- Inline field-level errors for form validation, shown adjacent to the relevant field.
- Page-level error states (e.g., failed data load) include a retry action.
- Toast/snackbar notifications for transient errors (e.g., failed auto-save) that don't require blocking the whole UI.

### 16.7 Success States

- Confirmation via toast/snackbar for quick actions (saved, deleted, moved).
- Celebratory but restrained micro-feedback for milestone actions (first resume completed, first interview session finished, ATS score crossing 80+) to reinforce engagement without feeling gimmicky.

### 16.8 Animations & Micro-Interactions

- Transitions kept under 300ms to feel responsive, not sluggish.
- Drag-and-drop interactions (resume sections, Kanban cards) provide clear visual affordance (drop-zone highlighting, card lift/shadow on drag).
- Score changes (ATS score before/after edits) animate incrementally rather than jumping instantly, to help users perceive cause-and-effect of their edits.
- All animations respect the OS-level "reduce motion" accessibility preference.

## 19. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Impact | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| **AI provider cost overrun** — heavy users consume disproportionate AI compute relative to their subscription price. | High (margin erosion) | Medium | AI credit system with hard per-plan caps (Section 5.10); usage anomaly monitoring (Section 5.11); ability to tune per-action credit costs without a full release cycle. |
| **AI output quality inconsistency** — generated cover letters/interview feedback occasionally feel generic or miss context. | Medium (user trust) | Medium | Prompt constrained to user-provided facts only (FR-COVER-07); question-flagging mechanism (Edge Case 41) feeds a quality-review loop; user can always manually edit generated content. |
| **ATS scoring accuracy skepticism** — users may distrust a score that doesn't match real-world outcomes. | Medium (perceived credibility) | Medium | Score always paired with specific, explainable reasoning (FR-ATS-06), never a bare number; scan history lets users see correlation between score improvements and their own edits over time. |
| **Low free-to-paid conversion** — generous free tier reduces upgrade incentive. | High (revenue) | Medium | Credit limits calibrated and monitored via KPI dashboard (Section 2.4); upgrade prompts triggered contextually at natural friction points (credit exhaustion, resume limit) rather than generically. |
| **Data privacy/regulatory non-compliance** — resume data contains sensitive PII across multiple jurisdictions. | High (legal exposure) | Low–Medium | Explicit data portability and deletion requirements (Section 6, Section 15); dedicated legal review flagged in Appendix A as an open dependency before international launch. |
| **Third-party AI provider outage** — core AI features become unavailable. | Medium (availability) | Low–Medium | Graceful degradation behavior defined in Section 11.2; clear user messaging rather than silent failure; architecture should not hard-couple non-AI features (resume editing, job tracking) to AI provider uptime. |
| **Resume parsing/import inaccuracy** — imported resumes populate incorrect profile data. | Medium (user frustration, data quality) | Medium | Parsed data always presented for user review/correction before saving (FR-PROF-11), never auto-committed silently. |
| **Concurrent editing data loss** — users editing the same resume across multiple tabs/devices lose work. | Medium (trust, support burden) | Low | Non-destructive conflict detection and resolution UX (Edge Case 20, Section 11.2), append-only version history as a safety net. |
| **Admin tooling misuse or error** — an admin action (refund, credit adjustment, suspension) is applied incorrectly. | Medium (financial/reputational) | Low | Mandatory reason codes and immutable audit logging (FR-ADMIN-02, FR-ADMIN-06); MFA-gated admin access. |

## Appendix A — Assumptions Log

Since no engineering team, budget, or technology stack was specified in the source brief, the following assumptions were made and should be validated with stakeholders before implementation begins:

1. The platform is a web-first SaaS product (desktop + mobile browser); native mobile apps are explicitly deferred to Future Scope.
2. Payment processing is assumed to use a third-party PCI-compliant processor (referenced generically as "Stripe" for concreteness); no card data is stored directly by the platform.
3. AI generation features (ATS scoring, cover letters, interview questions/evaluation) are assumed to be powered by a third-party large language model provider accessed via API, not a self-hosted model, at MVP stage.
4. Specific numeric plan limits (e.g., "3 ATS scans/month" on Free plan) are illustrative defaults for requirement-writing purposes and are configurable business parameters subject to pricing/business-model finalization.
5. "AI credits" are treated as an internal abstraction layer over underlying AI provider costs, allowing the business to tune pricing/margins without being tied 1:1 to raw API token costs.
6. English is the only supported language at MVP; localization is Phase 3+.
7. The Recruiter persona is explicitly out of scope for MVP functional requirements and is documented for context/future planning only.
8. Voice-based interview answers are deferred to Phase 2; MVP interview answers are text-only.
9. DOCX resume export is deferred to Phase 2; MVP resume export is PDF-only (DOCX is supported at MVP for cover letters only, per source brief).
10. Data retention/deletion timelines (e.g., 30-day trash for deleted resumes) are illustrative and must be finalized against actual legal/compliance requirements (GDPR/CCPA) for the platform's operating jurisdictions.
11. The system is assumed to target a global user base, requiring UTC-based server-side time handling and currency-agnostic billing display; specific regional compliance (e.g., India's DPDP Act, EU GDPR) requires dedicated legal review beyond this PRD's scope.
